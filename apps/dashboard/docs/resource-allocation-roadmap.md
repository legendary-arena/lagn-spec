## 1. Understanding the Budget

### What Does $200/Month Actually Buy?

The Claude Max 20x plan provides approximately **900 messages per 5-hour rolling window** — effectively near-continuous access throughout the workday. But messages aren't all equal. A simple question uses one message. A complex coding session with a large codebase pasted in uses more context and may yield fewer total interactions.

Rather than counting individual messages, it's more practical to think in terms of **work sessions** — focused blocks of time where you and Claude accomplish a specific task.

### Work Session Definitions

| Session Type | Description | Typical Duration | What Gets Done |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Planning Session** | Strategist or Architect work — specs, briefs, research | 30–60 min | 1 campaign brief, 1 feature spec, or 1 research deep-dive |
| **Build Session** | Builder or Creator work — writing code or content | 60–120 min | 1 feature built, 1 landing page written, or 1 email sequence drafted |
| **Review Session** | Inspector or Analyst work — reviewing code or metrics | 30–60 min | 1 code review, 1 campaign performance analysis, or 1 funnel audit |
| **Audit Session** | Evaluator or Auditor work — quarterly deep assessment | 90–120 min | 1 full department evaluation with scored report |

### Monthly Capacity Estimate

Assuming Jeff works on Legendary Arena **15–20 hours per month** (part-time, evenings and weekends):

| Item | Estimate |
|------|----------|
| Total hours available per month | 15–20 hours |
| Average session length | ~1 hour |
| **Estimated sessions per month** | **15–20 sessions** |
| Claude Max 20x capacity | More than sufficient for this workload |
| Effective cost per session | ~$10–13 per session |

This means each session should produce **at least $10 worth of progress** — a useful gut-check before starting any work.

### Allocation Rules

1. **No department gets zero sessions.** Every department needs at least one session per month to prevent blind spots.
2. **Buffer is sacred.** The 10% buffer covers emergencies, research, and unplanned spikes. Don't pre-allocate it.
3. **Rebalance quarterly.** After each quarterly scorecard, shift sessions toward the lowest-scoring departments.
4. **Track actual vs. planned.** If Builder sessions consistently take 3 hours instead of 1, you're under-budgeting that department.

## 3. The Sprint System

### What's a Sprint?

Borrow from agile software development: a **sprint** is a fixed time period (2 weeks) with a defined set of deliverables. At the start of each sprint, you decide exactly what gets done. At the end, you check what was actually completed.

### Sprint Structure (2-Week Cycle)

```
SPRINT PLANNING (30 min — start of Week 1)
  → Pick 4–6 tasks from the roadmap
  → Assign each to an agent/department
  → Estimate sessions needed

WEEK 1: Execute tasks 1–3
WEEK 2: Execute tasks 4–6

SPRINT REVIEW (30 min — end of Week 2)
  → What was completed?
  → What wasn't? Why?
  → What carries over to next sprint?
  → Update the progress tracker
```

## 7. Monthly Review Template

At the end of each month, the Orchestrator (Jeff) spends one session with Claude answering these questions:

### The Five Questions

**1. Did we hit our milestones?**
Review the phase milestones. Mark each as ✅ Done, 🔄 In Progress, or ❌ Missed. Missed milestones get re-scheduled or dropped (with a documented reason).

**2. Did we stay within budget allocation?**
Compare planned sessions per department vs. actual. If Builder consumed 8 sessions instead of 4, that's a signal — either the work was underestimated, or scope crept.

**3. What's our sprint velocity?**
Are we completing more or fewer tasks per sprint than last month? Velocity trending up = getting more efficient. Trending down = something is slowing us down (scope creep, unclear specs, burnout).

**4. What's our scorecard trend?**
Pull up the department scores. Which went up? Which went down? The lowest-scoring department gets extra sessions next month.

**5. Is the $200 generating $200+ in value?**
This is the no-margin-no-mission check. Is the work being done translating into something that will generate revenue? If three months have passed and there's no path to first sale, the allocation needs to shift hard toward Strategist and Creator.

### Monthly Review Deliverable

A one-page summary saved to `/docs/reviews/month-YYYY-MM.md` that captures the answers to the five questions and sets priorities for the next month. This becomes the historical record of the project's progress.
